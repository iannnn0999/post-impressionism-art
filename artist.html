Paul Cezanne

Paul Cézanne (1839–1906) was a French Post-Impressionist painter often called the “father of modern art.” He bridged Impressionism and Cubism by using bold colors, geometric shapes, and structured brushstrokes. Cézanne is best known for his still lifes of fruit, landscapes of Provence, and portraits. His style influenced artists like Picasso and Matisse, helping shape 20th-century modern art.

Van Gogh

Vincent van Gogh (1853–1890) was a Dutch Post-Impressionist painter known for his expressive brushwork, bold colors, and emotional intensity. Despite struggling with mental health and selling few works in his lifetime, he created over 2,000 artworks, including The Starry Night, Sunflowers, and Wheatfield with Crows. Today, he is celebrated as one of the most influential artists in history.

George Suerat

Georges Seurat (1859–1891) was a French Post-Impressionist painter best known for developing Pointillism, a technique of painting with tiny dots of pure color that blend in the viewer’s eye. His most famous work, A Sunday Afternoon on the Island of La Grande Jatte, is a landmark in modern art and shows his scientific approach to color and light.

Paul Gaugin

Paul Gauguin (1848–1903) was a French Post-Impressionist artist celebrated for his use of vivid colors and symbolic themes. He is best known for his paintings of Tahitian people and landscapes, which broke away from European artistic traditions.
